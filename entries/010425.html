Rhine Papyrus Problem 40

100 loaves of bread are to be divided among five men. The men's five shares of bread are to be in arithmetic progression, so that consecutive shares always differ by a fixed difference, or Δ Furthermore, the sum of the three largest shares is to be equal to seven times the sum of the two smallest shares. Find Δ and write it as an Egyptian fraction.

The Solution

Say that x is the amount of bread given to the man with the least bread, and d is the "fixed difference" between them. Then
(1)

x + (d + x) + (2d + x) +...+(4d + x) = 100

Then, " the sum of the three largest shares is to be equal to seven times the sum of the two smallest shares"

(4d+x)+(3d+x)+(2d+x) = 7(x+d+x), 9d+3x = 14x+7d 9d = 11x+7d 2d = 11x d = 11x/2

Now we can use our definition of d in terms of x to solve for x.

5x+10*(11x/2) = 100 5x+55x = 100 60x = 100 x = 100/60 x = 5/3 d = 55/6
