Egyptian Multiplication

Otto Neugebauer, in his The Exact Sciences in Antiquity gives the following "additive" method of multiplication that prevalent in Ancient Egypt (though he omits his sources).


Say we wish to multiply `17` and `23`. We note that `17=2^0+2^5` and process in this way


1 : 23 2 : 46 4 : 92 8 : 184 16 : 368


and write `17*23=368+23=391`


clearly this works. `p*q=\sum_{k=0}^{n}2^k*q`, where `2^(n+1)` is the least `n>q`. Expanding this expression by distributing q over the sum shows why it works.
