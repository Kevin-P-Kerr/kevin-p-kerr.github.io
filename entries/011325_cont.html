Recursive Root Extraction

While continuing to research ancient methods of root extraction and their investigations into irrational numbers, I came accross the following, "continued fraction" inspired identity.
`\sqrt{x} = 1 + (\sqrt{x}-1) = 1+(x-1)/(1+\sqrt{x})`
It's straightforward to write this as a scheme one-liner" Computing the square root of two, we get, after n rounds
`1 : 4/3`
`2 : 10/7`
`3: 24/17`
After 100 rounds, we get `323466434400377142162623973268164663418/228725309250740208744750893347264645481` which is good through 76 decimal places.
It's a little funny that this algorithm will never find the roots of perfect squares: It's first approximation to `sqrt{25}` is `25/13`, it's 100th iteration gives `7730662810980169978223422948766613105498644883775/1546132562196033990574082188840405015112916155251` which is about 5.000000000000000016.
