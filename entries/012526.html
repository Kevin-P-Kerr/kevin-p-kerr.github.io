Solving Linear Equations

Consider the following equation `5x+7y=27`
Say we wish to find positive integer solutions. We can follow the approch elaborated by Euler and which I found in a short text book on continued fractions (n.b. this approach does not use continued fractions--that is elaborated in a further chapter.) We begin by searching for integer solutions, positive or negative, and then restrict our search to positive solutions. `x=5-y+(2(1-y)/5)`
let `z=2(1-y)/5`, then `x=5-y+z`
So that if y is an intger, so will be x.

`5z=2-2y`, therefore `y=(2-5z)/2=1-2z-z/2`
If we let `m=z/2` then `z=2m` and
`y=1-5m`, `x=4+7m`
Then `5x+7y=20+35m+7-35m=27` confirms our work thus far.
Finally, since want to find instances where `x,y>0` we consider these inequalities
`4+7m>0`
`1-5m>0`, and find
`m>(-1)20/35` and `m<7/35`
There is one integer between these two fractions, namely 0. so x=4,y=1 gives the only integer solution to our problem.
